AI-safe boundaries

AI agent use cases

AI agents can help partners draft scripts, summarize readiness, prepare CRM notes, classify inquiries, and explain resources. They should not make funding decisions, approval claims, rate quotes, underwriting judgments, or commission promises.

Recommended GPT/action workflows

Partner Profile Helper

Helps a partner choose partner type, channel, audience, and onboarding path.

Lead Intake Assistant

Transforms permitted business context into clean intake notes and missing-info questions.

Compliance Copy Reviewer

Flags restricted phrases and rewrites copy into safer funding-readiness language.

CRM Note Formatter

Formats partner notes into CRM-ready summaries without inventing facts or statuses.

Agent rules

  • Do not identify a business as approved, fundable, eligible, or declined unless the real system provides that status.
  • Do not ask for bank logins, SSNs, full tax IDs, account numbers, or private documents inside a chat action.
  • Do not invent lender names, offers, rates, terms, timelines, or commissions.
  • Always preserve partner ID, source, timestamp, and consent context when creating backend records.
  • Return structured errors when a live endpoint rejects a request.
  • Use educational language: readiness, options, review path, documents, next steps.

Human review checkpoints

  • Partner copy before publishing.
  • New marketplace offers before promotion.
  • Lead submissions with missing consent or unusual risk indicators.
  • Commission estimates before sharing with partners.
  • Any AI-generated claims that sound like approval, eligibility, or guaranteed income.
